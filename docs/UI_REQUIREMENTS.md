# UI Requirements

## Brand Direction

La Mesa should feel like the official ISD Founder 12 cohort portal for Costa Rica.

The interface should communicate:
- Selective access.
- Founder seriousness.
- ISD credibility.
- Costa Rica founder community.
- Warmth and trust.
- Momentum toward prototype readiness.

Avoid:
- Generic SaaS coldness.
- Dating-app gimmicks.
- Freelancer marketplace visuals.
- Overly academic visuals.
- Startup-bro gradients everywhere.
- Childish Latin stereotypes.
- Visual clutter.

## Visual Feel

Recommended qualities:
- Premium
- Warm
- Calm
- Selective
- Founder-centered
- Community-connected
- Strategic
- Execution-oriented

The product should feel like a serious founder has been invited into an ISD room, not like they are browsing an open marketplace.

## Suggested Color Direction

Keep the ISD navy, mint, teal, and clean light surfaces already in the product.

Use:
- Deep navy for authority.
- Mint/teal for access, momentum, and AI support.
- Light neutral backgrounds for clarity.
- Dark green or muted accent colors for Costa Rica/community warmth.

Do not make the app one-note or overly decorative.

## Landing Page Structure

### Hero

Headline direction:

> Build the future together.

Subheadline direction:

> Apply for Founder 12, the ISD cohort for founders in Costa Rica moving from proof of concept to capital readiness.

Buttons:
- Apply for Founder 12
- Join the Builder Network

### Founder 12 Offer

Explain:
- Selective 12-founder cohort.
- ISD review.
- Founders Coffee.
- AI sessions.
- ISD member privileges.
- Team formation support.
- Prototype readiness.

### Why It Exists

Explain:
- Serious founders need more than a form.
- Ideas need structure, community, and trusted support.
- Builders should support accepted ventures, not random listings.
- ISD creates the trusted table.

### How It Works

1. Apply for Founder 12.
2. AI generates a readiness report and role map.
3. ISD reviews and selects 12 founders.
4. Accepted founders unlock Founders Coffee, ISD privileges, and AI sessions.
5. ISD coordinates builder support and prototype readiness.

### What Accepted Founders Unlock

Cards or modules:
- Founders Coffee.
- ISD member privileges.
- AI sessions.
- Builder matching support.
- 30-day roadmap.
- Demo readiness.

### Builder Network

Explain:
- Builders apply to support Founder 12 ventures.
- Builders are approved by ISD.
- Builders are matched manually by admin.
- Builders do not browse all ventures in MVP.

### CTA Section

> Ready to apply for Founder 12?

Buttons:
- Apply for Founder 12
- Join the Builder Network

## App UI

### Founder Applicant Dashboard

Should show:
- Application status.
- Review stage.
- AI readiness score.
- Venture summary.
- Next step from ISD.
- Message that accepted-founder benefits unlock only if selected.

### Accepted Founder Dashboard

Should show:
- "Accepted to Founder 12" state.
- Founders Coffee module.
- ISD member privileges module.
- AI sessions module.
- Team formation module.
- Venture roadmap.
- Progress updates.

### Builder Dashboard

Should show:
- Builder Network profile status.
- Approval status.
- Assigned Founder 12 ventures only.
- Role and availability summary.

### Admin Dashboard

Should become the Cohort Command Center.

Should show:
- Applications received.
- Shortlisted founders.
- Accepted founders, with 12-seat capacity indicator.
- Not selected/deferred founders.
- Builders awaiting approval.
- Accepted ventures needing team formation.
- Teams formed.
- Demo-ready ventures.

## Card Patterns

### Founder Application Card

Use for admin review.

Should show:
- Founder name.
- Venture name.
- One-sentence idea.
- Readiness score.
- Founder 12 fit indicator.
- Track/category.
- Stage.
- Status.
- Review action.

### Accepted Founder Card

Should show:
- Founder name.
- Venture name.
- Founder 12 badge.
- Founders Coffee status.
- AI session status.
- Team formation status.
- Demo readiness signal.

### Builder Match Card

Use only inside admin team formation.

Should show:
- Builder name.
- Recommended role.
- Match score.
- Skills.
- Availability.
- Fit reasons.
- Risk notes.
- Assign button.

## Form UX

Forms should be guided and broken into sections.

Founder 12 application sections:
1. Founder basics.
2. Venture basics.
3. Problem and users.
4. Stage and scope.
5. Team needs.
6. Founder commitment.
7. Founder 12 fit.
8. Founders Coffee / Costa Rica participation.
9. Review and submit.

Builder profile sections:
1. Personal basics.
2. Skills.
3. Roles.
4. Availability.
5. Interests.
6. Links and bio.

## Tone

Use direct, warm, selective language.

Good:
- "Apply for Founder 12."
- "Your application is under ISD review."
- "You have been accepted to Founder 12."
- "Founders Coffee access unlocked."
- "AI session access."
- "ISD member privileges."
- "What are you building?"
- "Why is now the right time?"
- "What support would help you move faster?"
- "How many hours can you commit?"

Avoid:
- "Browse marketplace."
- "Swipe to match."
- "Bid on projects."
- "Synergize innovation stakeholders."
- "Leverage ecosystemic value creation."
- "Disrupt team formation pipelines."

No jargon soup. The product should sound like a serious founder program with taste.
