# UI Requirements

## Brand Direction

La Mesa should feel like a modern AI startup with Latin community warmth.

Avoid:
- Generic SaaS coldness
- Overly academic visuals
- Startup-bro gradients everywhere
- Childish Latin stereotypes
- Visual clutter

## Visual Feel

Recommended qualities:
- Warm
- Calm
- Grounded
- Clean
- Human
- Strategic
- Founder-friendly

## Suggested Color Direction

Use warm, earthy, premium colors:

- Warm ivory / off-white background
- Deep charcoal text
- Terracotta accent
- Coffee brown
- Muted gold or sand
- Deep green as optional accent

Do not overuse bright colors.

## Landing Page Structure

### Hero
Headline:
> Bring your idea to the table.

Subheadline:
> La Mesa helps founders, students, and builders form teams, map the roles they need, and move from idea to prototype.

Buttons:
- Apply as Founder
- Join as Builder

### Problem Section
Explain:
- Ideas need teams.
- Talent is scattered.
- Startup communities need structure.
- Students need real projects.

### Solution Section
Explain:
- Guided applications
- AI role mapping
- Builder profiles
- Admin-assisted matching
- 30-day prototype roadmap

### How It Works Section
1. Submit your idea
2. AI maps the roles
3. Builders are matched
4. Teams follow a 30-day roadmap
5. Projects move toward prototype/demo day

### Who It Is For
- Founders
- Students
- Developers
- Designers
- AI builders
- Blockchain builders
- Robotics builders
- Startup communities
- University innovation programs

### CTA Section
> Ready to bring your idea to the table?

Buttons:
- Apply as Founder
- Join as Builder

## App UI

### Dashboard
Should be simple and role-specific.

Founder dashboard:
- Project cards
- Status
- Readiness score
- Team status
- Next steps

Builder dashboard:
- Profile status
- Assigned projects
- Skills summary
- Availability

Admin dashboard:
- Pending projects
- Pending builders
- Projects needing matches
- Teams formed
- Prototype-ready projects

## Form UX

Forms should be guided and broken into sections.

Founder application sections:
1. Project basics
2. Problem and users
3. Stage and scope
4. Team needs
5. Goals and expectations
6. Review and submit

Builder profile sections:
1. Personal basics
2. Skills
3. Roles
4. Availability
5. Interests
6. Links and bio

## Tone

Use direct, warm language.

Good:
- "What are you building?"
- "Who is this for?"
- "What kind of team do you need?"
- "What can you help build?"
- "How many hours can you commit?"

Avoid:
- "Synergize innovation stakeholders"
- "Leverage ecosystemic value creation"
- "Disrupt team formation pipelines"

No jargon soup. The product should sound like a sharp founder, not a conference brochure.
