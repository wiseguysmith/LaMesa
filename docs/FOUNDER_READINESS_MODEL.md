# Founder Readiness Model

## Purpose

The Founder Readiness Model helps ISD and founders understand whether a project is ready for team formation and a 30-day prototype sprint.

The score should be visible to founders, but it should feel like coaching rather than judgment.

The founder-facing artifact is the Founder Readiness Report.

## Founder Readiness Report

The report should include:
- Overall readiness score
- Category scores
- Short explanation
- Biggest strengths
- Biggest risks
- Recommended next steps
- Missing information
- Role map
- Suggested team size
- 30-day roadmap

## Tone

The report should be direct, constructive, and founder-friendly.

Good language:
- Your project is promising but not yet team-ready.
- Your project is ready for matching once these gaps are clarified.
- Your strongest signal is founder-market fit.
- Your biggest risk is unclear target users.

Avoid:
- Cold grading language
- Generic startup advice
- Excessive praise
- Harsh rejection language
- AI-sounding filler

## Scoring Criteria

### Seriousness and Consistency

Measures whether the founder appears committed, thoughtful, and willing to follow through.

Signals:
- Clear goals
- Real availability
- Specific next steps
- Evidence of prior effort
- Consistent answers across the application

### Clarity of Problem

Measures whether the founder understands the problem being solved.

Signals:
- Specific problem statement
- Clear pain point
- Evidence that the problem matters
- Avoids vague solution-first framing

### Market Ambition

Measures whether the idea has meaningful growth, revenue, or impact potential.

Signals:
- Large or urgent market
- Clear customer segment
- Scalable opportunity
- Meaningful community or economic impact

### Technical Feasibility

Measures whether the project can be prototyped with available resources in 30 days.

Signals:
- MVP can be scoped
- Technical needs are understandable
- Dependencies are realistic
- No excessive complexity for first prototype

### Coachability

Measures whether the founder seems open to feedback, iteration, and focus.

Signals:
- Acknowledges unknowns
- Names risks honestly
- Accepts need to narrow scope
- Shows willingness to validate assumptions

### Founder-Market Fit

Measures whether the founder has a credible connection to the problem.

Signals:
- Personal experience
- Professional experience
- Community access
- Customer insight
- Domain credibility

### Ability to Recruit

Measures whether the founder can attract and work with collaborators.

Signals:
- Clear team needs
- Realistic collaboration expectations
- Strong mission
- Communicates why builders should care

### Community Impact

Measures whether the project can strengthen local or target communities.

Signals:
- Meaningful benefit to users
- Local ecosystem relevance
- Inclusion or access benefits
- Clear social or economic contribution

### Speed of Execution

Measures whether the founder can move quickly through validation and building.

Signals:
- Clear timeline
- Focused MVP
- Availability
- Willingness to make decisions

### Prototype Potential in 30 Days

Measures whether the project can produce a credible demo in the Table cycle.

Signals:
- Prototype can be defined
- Initial feature set is small enough
- Team needs are realistic
- Demo outcome is clear

## Suggested Weighting

- Problem clarity: 15%
- Founder-market fit: 15%
- Prototype potential: 15%
- Technical feasibility: 10%
- Seriousness and consistency: 10%
- Ability to recruit: 10%
- Speed of execution: 10%
- Market ambition: 5%
- Coachability: 5%
- Community impact: 5%

Weights can change by track.

For example:
- Community Impact may weight impact higher.
- Robotics may weight technical feasibility higher.
- Student Founder may weight coachability and speed higher.

## Readiness Bands

### 85-100: Table-Ready

The project is highly ready for team formation.

Founder-facing language:
- Your project is ready for consideration and strong enough to enter matching if selected.

### 70-84: Promising

The project has strong signals but needs clarification or scope control.

Founder-facing language:
- Your project is promising. Clarifying these gaps will improve your chance of selection.

### 55-69: Needs Focus

The project may be viable but is not yet clear enough for immediate team formation.

Founder-facing language:
- Your project has potential, but it needs sharper problem definition, user clarity, or MVP scope.

### Below 55: Not Yet Ready

The project is too unclear, broad, or infeasible for the current Table.

Founder-facing language:
- Your project is not ready for this Table yet. Use the feedback to refine and reapply.

## Admin Use

Admins should use the score as decision support, not as an automatic selection rule.

Admins should be able to override AI scoring based on:
- Interview results
- Founder credibility
- Strategic track priorities
- Builder availability
- Community value
- ISD judgment

## Founder Visibility

Founders should see:
- Overall score
- Category scores
- Strengths
- Risks
- Next steps
- Missing information

Founders should not initially see:
- Admin-only notes
- Internal ranking against other founders
- Sensitive match strategy

## AI Role

AI should act as:
- Founder application reviewer
- Idea clarity coach
- Role/team architect
- Demo Day readiness coach

AI should not:
- Select founders automatically
- Reject founders automatically
- Make legal, equity, or compensation judgments
- Promise acceptance
