# Founder Readiness Model

## Purpose

The Founder Readiness Model helps ISD understand whether a founder and venture are a strong fit for Founder 12.

The score should support selection and coaching. It should not automatically accept or reject anyone.

The founder-facing artifact is the Founder Readiness Report.

## Founder Readiness Report

The report should include:
- Overall readiness score.
- Category scores.
- Founder 12 fit notes.
- Short explanation.
- Biggest strengths.
- Biggest risks.
- Recommended next steps.
- Missing information.
- Role map.
- Suggested team size.
- 30-day roadmap.

## Tone

The report should be direct, constructive, and founder-friendly.

Good language:
- Your venture is promising, but your first user segment needs sharper definition.
- You appear ready for Founder 12 if you can commit consistent weekly time.
- Your strongest signal is founder-market fit.
- Your biggest risk is an unclear prototype scope.

Avoid:
- Cold grading language.
- Generic startup advice.
- Excessive praise.
- Harsh rejection language.
- AI-sounding filler.

## Scoring Criteria

### Founder Commitment

Measures whether the founder appears committed, thoughtful, and able to participate in Founder 12.

Signals:
- Real weekly availability.
- Clear goals.
- Specific next steps.
- Evidence of prior effort.
- Consistent answers across the application.
- Ability to participate in Founders Coffee or cohort touchpoints.

### Clarity Of Problem

Measures whether the founder understands the problem being solved.

Signals:
- Specific problem statement.
- Clear pain point.
- Evidence that the problem matters.
- Avoids vague solution-first framing.

### User Clarity

Measures whether the founder knows who the first users/customers are.

Signals:
- Specific first user segment.
- Credible access to users.
- Clear use case.
- Evidence of user pain.

### Founder-Market Fit

Measures whether the founder has a credible connection to the problem.

Signals:
- Personal experience.
- Professional experience.
- Community access.
- Customer insight.
- Domain credibility.

### Founder 12 Fit

Measures whether the founder is likely to benefit from and contribute to the Founder 12 cohort.

Signals:
- Coachability.
- Community orientation.
- Willingness to show up.
- Clear reason for applying.
- Alignment with ISD support.
- Ability to use AI sessions, Founders Coffee, and builder support.

### Technical Feasibility

Measures whether the venture can be prototyped with available resources.

Signals:
- MVP can be scoped.
- Technical needs are understandable.
- Dependencies are realistic.
- No excessive complexity for first prototype.

### Ability To Recruit / Use Support

Measures whether the founder can attract and work with collaborators.

Signals:
- Clear team needs.
- Realistic collaboration expectations.
- Strong mission.
- Communicates why builders should care.
- Openness to admin-guided team formation.

### Market Or Impact Potential

Measures whether the idea has meaningful growth, revenue, or impact potential.

Signals:
- Large or urgent market.
- Clear customer segment.
- Scalable opportunity.
- Meaningful community or economic impact.

### Speed Of Execution

Measures whether the founder can move quickly through validation and building.

Signals:
- Clear timeline.
- Focused MVP.
- Availability.
- Willingness to make decisions.

### Prototype Potential

Measures whether the venture can produce a credible demo or prototype.

Signals:
- Prototype can be defined.
- Initial feature set is small enough.
- Team needs are realistic.
- Demo outcome is clear.

## Suggested Weighting

- Founder commitment: 15%
- Founder 12 fit: 15%
- Problem clarity: 15%
- User clarity: 10%
- Founder-market fit: 10%
- Prototype potential: 15%
- Technical feasibility: 10%
- Ability to recruit/use support: 5%
- Speed of execution: 3%
- Market or impact potential: 2%

Weights can change after the first cohort.

## Readiness Bands

### 85-100: Strong Founder 12 Candidate

Founder-facing language:
- Your application shows strong Founder 12 fit and clear prototype potential.

### 70-84: Promising Candidate

Founder-facing language:
- Your application is promising. Clarifying these gaps will improve your Founder 12 readiness.

### 55-69: Needs Focus

Founder-facing language:
- Your venture has potential, but it needs sharper problem definition, user clarity, commitment, or MVP scope.

### Below 55: Not Yet Ready

Founder-facing language:
- Your application is not ready for Founder 12 yet. Use the feedback to refine and reapply.

## Admin Use

Admins should use the score as decision support, not as an automatic selection rule.

Admins should be able to override AI scoring based on:
- Interview results.
- Founder credibility.
- Strategic ISD priorities.
- Founders Coffee participation.
- Community value.
- Builder availability.
- ISD judgment.

## Founder Visibility

Founders should see:
- Overall score.
- Category scores.
- Founder 12 fit notes.
- Strengths.
- Risks.
- Next steps.
- Missing information.

Founders should not initially see:
- Admin-only notes.
- Internal ranking against other founders.
- Sensitive match strategy.
- Selection committee discussion.

## ISD Review Questions

AI should generate review questions for admins.

Examples:
- Can the founder commit consistent weekly time during Founder 12?
- Can the founder attend Founders Coffee or core cohort touchpoints?
- What is the smallest prototype that proves the venture?
- Which first user group will the founder validate with?
- What support from ISD would most improve the founder's odds?

## AI Role

AI should act as:
- Founder application reviewer.
- Idea clarity coach.
- Founder 12 fit analyst.
- Role/team architect.
- Prototype readiness coach.

AI should not:
- Select founders automatically.
- Reject founders automatically.
- Make legal, equity, or compensation judgments.
- Promise acceptance.
